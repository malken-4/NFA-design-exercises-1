Problem 20 gave me the most trouble because at first, I was trying to combine the states to make a 2D NFA diagram, but then I realized I could just combine the two parts of the problem using epsilon. I didn't necessarily avoid choosing harder ones; I just tried to do an even spread of different types of problems. However, most of them turned out to be simpler, and I wish I had done more of the problems from #20 up.

### Which problem(s) surprised you with a "gold-st-ring"? Which next state(s) did you not account for in the subset of next states? why? How to make sure you avoid such errors in your future flight/traffic/compiler state controller tasks, or in the near future, the course projects/exams?

While completing these problems, I didn't encounter a "gold-st-ring". I hand-drew each of my NFA diagrams before putting them into JFLAP, and while drawing them, I tested along the way with strings already. So by the time the NFAs were recreated in JFLAP, they have accounted for all strings and therefore no suprises occured.
